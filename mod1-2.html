Module 1 • Foundation

Foundations of Merindilogun Divination

Module 1 establishes the sacred framework of the work. The student learns what the system is, how to regard the shells, how to approach the mat, and how to prepare the body, mind, and intention before casting.

  1. What is Merindilogun
    The sixteen-cowrie divination system used within Orisha traditions.
  2. The role of the diviner
    Alignment with Ori, reverence for Orisha, and acting as witness to destiny.
  3. The divination mat
    The mat as sacred boundary, ritual field, and ordered spiritual space.
  4. Orientation and direction
    Establishing directional awareness before beginning the cast.
  5. Preparing the cowries
    Understanding the 16 shells, ritual care, and respectful handling.
  6. The language of the shells
    Face-up and face-down as the basis of divinatory pattern.
  7. First casting practice
    Hand position, invocation, and disciplined casting.
Module 1 Outcome
Student can identify the 16 shells.
Student understands face-up vs face-down shell states.
Student can prepare the mat and begin with order.
Student can perform an initial respectful cast.
Module 2 • Recognition

Reading the Shell Pattern

Module 2 begins the interpretive threshold. The student learns to count the number of open shells, identify the resulting pattern, and understand that the visible count opens the door toward Odu, Ire, Ibi, and message.

  1. Count the open shells
    The visible openings become the first readable signal.
  2. Recognize the pattern
    Each count corresponds to a recognizable energetic form.
  3. Enter the Odu gateway
    The shell result becomes an entrance into deeper wisdom.
  4. Confirm Ire or Ibi
    The result is further clarified by beneficial or obstructive condition.
  5. Begin interpretation
    The student starts linking count, condition, and message.
Module 2 Outcome
Student can count open-shell results accurately.
Student can identify the basic pattern or entry point.
Student begins distinguishing Ire from Ibi.
Student understands that counting leads into Odu interpretation.
